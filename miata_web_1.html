Why should you buy a Mazda Miata?

January 17th, 2023


When you hear someone talk about Mazda, what do you usually think? Maybe the reliable and popular CX-5? Or perhaps the modern and versatile Mazda Speed? Ask the general population, and these are usually something similar to the answers you'll recieve. Ask most car enthusiast, and their answer will be the MX-5 Miata.

Now, if you have a general understanding of iconic cars, and or have seen any of the Fast And Furious Movies, I know what you're thinking already. What about the RX-7? That's a good point, and one many would make. The RX series pionereed rotary engines, and were the first company to do so. But this isn't a documentary on the RX series, this is a praise to the genius design and practically of the Mazda MX-5 Miata.

A look back on the 90's, the MX-5s debut.

As we begin this anaylzation on one of the most iconic sport cars, there is no other place to start except looking at the father. That would be no other than the late Mazda design team member, Shunji Tanaka. Tanaka was actually the chief designer on the production line in his time. He was tasked with building a design that used both American and Japanese idea's and motion, and man did he deliver. "I thought of a wild animal when it's hunting, running fast and accurate, and for silence, the tranquility of nature. I wanted the car to melt into the scenery, reflecting the light over its curved surfaces."

A little too spiratual for me, but the Miata did indeed run accurate, cutting corners so fast there'd be no time for the light to reflect. More on this later, instead, let's take a look at the history of the MX-5 Miata.


Why you should buy a Mazda Miata.

Originally, I was going to go answer this question in the opening paragraph. But I came to the conclusion that the history and evolution of the Miata needs to be addressed first. Tanaka's dream for the Miata, the evoultion, the changes and benefits of each generation, hell even the images are needed to help you, the reader understand why this car is so great. If you happen to still be asking that question, then for one, I need to work on my developing skills. For two, let me just answer it simply in the case of that. Because they combine reliabilty, handling, precision, and enjoyability all into one small car.



I won't delve too deep into the whole reliablilty thing, since well, if you like sport cars that's probably not your first thought. The Miata is a rear wheel drive car, quite different than the typical front wheel drive. You can get sideways in this fu*$ing thing if you're daring enough! Ever since the NA, the Miata has had some of this best brakes, handling, and precision. It's nature is to simply be fun to drive. I don't thinks there's any better way to describe it.
